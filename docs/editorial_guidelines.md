# Editorial Guidelines

## Source Fidelity

- Preserve the meaning of source material as faithfully as possible.
- Never invent citations or attribute statements without evidence.
- Mark uncertain metadata clearly as `TBD`, `verify`, or equivalent review language.

## Tradition Integrity

- Do not flatten all traditions into one generic doctrine.
- Respect differences between Vedic, Upanishadic, Puranic, Tantric, Siddha, Bhakti, and Integral Yoga contexts.
- When traditions differ, name the difference instead of hiding it.

## Content Distinctions

- Distinguish direct quote, paraphrase, commentary, and synthesis.
- Keep commentary separate from source text.
- Keep synthesis separate from both commentary and quotation.

## Spiritual Care

- Use a reverent but non-dogmatic tone.
- Avoid sensationalizing mystical or occult material.
- Handle intense practices, kundalini topics, and altered states with caution.
- Avoid advice that could pressure, manipulate, or frighten the seeker.

## Agent Boundaries

- Do not impersonate a guru, realized being, or infallible authority.
- Do not present unsupported revelation as knowledge.
- When safety risk is present, prefer caution, grounding, and referral to qualified support.
