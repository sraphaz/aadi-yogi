# Deities

## Agni

- Traditions: Vedic
- Symbolic field: fire, offering, aspiration
- Use in guidance: inner flame, sincerity, sacrificial action
- Cautions: keep ritual specifics separate from symbolic use

## Indra

- Traditions: Vedic
- Symbolic field: illumined force, leadership, victory over obstruction
- Use in guidance: can support readings on courage and luminous power
- Cautions: avoid oversimplified myth reduction

## Soma

- Traditions: Vedic
- Symbolic field: delight, offering, sacred nourishment
- Use in guidance: points toward refined delight and sacred participation
- Cautions: avoid speculative certainty

## Surya

- Traditions: Vedic, Puranic
- Symbolic field: light, truth, vision
- Use in guidance: supports clarity, illumination, right seeing
- Cautions: distinguish symbolic, devotional, and ritual contexts

## Saraswati

- Traditions: Vedic, Puranic
- Symbolic field: knowledge, speech, inspiration
- Use in guidance: supports learning, mantra, and clear expression
- Cautions: do not sentimentalize or flatten meanings

## Shiva

- Traditions: Shaiva, Puranic, Tantric
- Symbolic field: stillness, transformation, destruction of ignorance
- Use in guidance: useful for silence, dissolution, and ascetic force
- Cautions: avoid glamorizing destructive imagery

## Vishnu

- Traditions: Vaishnava, Puranic
- Symbolic field: preservation, order, sustaining divinity
- Use in guidance: supports stability, protection, and cosmic maintenance
- Cautions: keep sectarian nuance intact

## Krishna

- Traditions: Vaishnava, Bhagavad Gita, Bhakti
- Symbolic field: divine personhood, guidance, love, action
- Use in guidance: central for devotion, dharma, and divine friendship
- Cautions: cite clearly when drawing from Gita versus later Bhakti literature

## Rama

- Traditions: Vaishnava, Ramayana
- Symbolic field: dharma, kingship, fidelity, courage
- Use in guidance: supports discussions of duty and nobility
- Cautions: handle moral complexity carefully

## Devi

- Traditions: Shakta, Puranic
- Symbolic field: Divine Mother, force, protection, creativity
- Use in guidance: helpful for devotion, protection, and transformation themes
- Cautions: keep symbolic richness without reducing to abstraction

## Kali

- Traditions: Shakta, Tantric
- Symbolic field: fierce grace, cutting through ego, time
- Use in guidance: may illuminate intense transformation themes
- Cautions: do not encourage reckless attraction to intensity

## Lakshmi

- Traditions: Vaishnava, Puranic
- Symbolic field: harmony, abundance, beauty
- Use in guidance: useful for balance, gratitude, and right relation to prosperity
- Cautions: avoid prosperity-only simplifications

## Durga

- Traditions: Shakta, Puranic
- Symbolic field: protection, strength, overcoming hostile forces
- Use in guidance: supports courage and protective invocation
- Cautions: keep psychological and devotional uses distinct

## Ganesha

- Traditions: broad Hindu traditions
- Symbolic field: beginnings, thresholds, obstacle-clearing, intelligence
- Use in guidance: supports transitional moments and opening movements
- Cautions: avoid formulaic "remove all obstacles" claims

## Skanda

- Traditions: Shaiva, Tamil devotional streams
- Symbolic field: warrior energy, clarity, spiritual force
- Use in guidance: useful for courage and disciplined advance
- Cautions: do not glorify aggression

## Hanuman

- Traditions: Ramayana, Bhakti
- Symbolic field: devotion, strength, service, humility
- Use in guidance: excellent for selfless service and faithful action
- Cautions: keep symbolism grounded in source context
