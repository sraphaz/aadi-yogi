# Concepts

## Brahman

- Traditions: Upanishadic, Vedantic, Integral Yoga
- Short definition: Ultimate reality, infinite being-consciousness beyond limitation.
- Related terms: Atman, Sat, Chit, Ananda
- Use in guidance: Helps frame questions about the Absolute and unity.
- Cautions: Do not reduce to an abstract slogan detached from lived practice.

## Atman

- Traditions: Upanishadic, Vedantic, Gita
- Short definition: The Self beyond ego and surface personality.
- Related terms: Brahman, Jivatman, Purusha
- Use in guidance: Useful when distinguishing enduring Self from changing experience.
- Cautions: Do not flatten distinct traditions into one identical doctrine of self.

## Purusha

- Traditions: Samkhya, Gita, Yoga
- Short definition: Conscious being or witness principle distinct from nature.
- Related terms: Prakriti, witness consciousness, Atman
- Use in guidance: Clarifies detachment, witnessing, and non-identification.
- Cautions: Explain context because meanings vary across schools.

## Prakriti

- Traditions: Samkhya, Gita, Yoga
- Short definition: Nature, becoming, or the dynamic field of qualities and processes.
- Related terms: Purusha, gunas, Shakti
- Use in guidance: Supports explanations of habit, action, and energetic movement.
- Cautions: Avoid fatalistic use that erases agency or conscious participation.

## Ishwara

- Traditions: Yoga, Vedanta, Bhakti
- Short definition: Lord, Divine Being, or personal aspect of the Supreme.
- Related terms: Brahman, Krishna, Shiva, Devi
- Use in guidance: Supports devotional and surrender-oriented responses.
- Cautions: Name the tradition-specific sense being used.

## Shakti

- Traditions: Shakta, Tantra, Integral Yoga
- Short definition: Divine force, conscious energy, or power of manifestation.
- Related terms: Devi, Prakriti, tapas
- Use in guidance: Useful in discussions of transformation, force, and practice energy.
- Cautions: Do not romanticize forceful experience or unsafe energetic practice.

## Maya

- Traditions: Vedanta, later philosophical usage
- Short definition: Power of appearance, measure, or cosmic veiling depending on context.
- Related terms: Brahman, Lila, avidya
- Use in guidance: Helps explain illusion, appearance, and ignorance with nuance.
- Cautions: Avoid simplistic "everything is illusion" dismissiveness.

## Lila

- Traditions: Vedantic, Bhakti, Puranic
- Short definition: Divine play or meaningful cosmic unfolding.
- Related terms: Maya, Ishwara, creation
- Use in guidance: Can open a wider view on life, symbol, and divine action.
- Cautions: Do not use to trivialize suffering.

## Sat

- Traditions: Upanishadic, Vedantic, Integral Yoga
- Short definition: Pure being or truth of existence.
- Related terms: Chit, Ananda, Sachchidananda
- Use in guidance: Grounds questions about reality and essential existence.
- Cautions: Avoid empty metaphysical jargon.

## Chit

- Traditions: Upanishadic, Vedantic, Integral Yoga
- Short definition: Consciousness or consciousness-force depending on context.
- Related terms: Sat, Ananda, Chit-Shakti
- Use in guidance: Useful for consciousness-centered explanations.
- Cautions: Clarify when consciousness means awareness versus force.

## Ananda

- Traditions: Upanishadic, Vedantic, Bhakti, Integral Yoga
- Short definition: Delight, bliss, or inherent divine joy.
- Related terms: Sat, Chit, Sachchidananda
- Use in guidance: Helps distinguish deep delight from emotional excitement.
- Cautions: Do not promise bliss as an immediate emotional state.

## Sachchidananda

- Traditions: Vedantic, Integral Yoga
- Short definition: Being-consciousness-delight as a unified expression of the Divine.
- Related terms: Sat, Chit, Ananda
- Use in guidance: Supports integral explanations of ultimate reality.
- Cautions: Use explanation, not unexplained terminology alone.

## Agni

- Traditions: Vedic, symbolic yogic usage
- Short definition: Fire, sacrificial principle, and inner flame of aspiration.
- Related terms: aspiration, tapas, offering
- Use in guidance: Powerful symbol for sincere upward movement.
- Cautions: Distinguish symbolic use from ritual specificity.

## Soma

- Traditions: Vedic
- Short definition: Sacred delight, offering, or sacramental principle with layered meanings.
- Related terms: Agni, delight, offering
- Use in guidance: Useful in symbolic and Vedic interpretive contexts.
- Cautions: Avoid confident reduction to one historical or esoteric reading.

## Dharma

- Traditions: Gita, Hindu traditions, broader Indian thought
- Short definition: Law, right order, duty, or truth of one's being depending on context.
- Related terms: karma, svadharma, order
- Use in guidance: Helps frame right action and alignment.
- Cautions: Do not weaponize duty against conscience or safety.

## Karma

- Traditions: broad Indian traditions
- Short definition: Action and consequence across inner and outer life.
- Related terms: dharma, action, samskara
- Use in guidance: Supports responsibility and learning.
- Cautions: Avoid deterministic or blame-based karma claims.

## Bhakti

- Traditions: Bhakti, Gita, Puranic
- Short definition: Devotion, loving relation, and heart-offering to the Divine.
- Related terms: surrender, love, Ishwara
- Use in guidance: Supports devotional openings and heart-centered practice.
- Cautions: Do not confuse devotion with dependency or coercion.

## Jnana

- Traditions: Vedanta, Gita
- Short definition: Knowledge, insight, or spiritual discernment into reality.
- Related terms: viveka, Atman, Brahman
- Use in guidance: Helps with philosophical questions and discrimination.
- Cautions: Avoid making jnana merely intellectual abstraction.

## Tapas

- Traditions: Vedic, yogic, Integral Yoga
- Short definition: Heat of concentration, disciplined force, or transformative intensity.
- Related terms: Agni, austerity, aspiration
- Use in guidance: Supports disciplined effort and transformative intent.
- Cautions: Warn against harsh self-violence or egoic severity.

## Sadhana

- Traditions: broad yogic and devotional traditions
- Short definition: Spiritual practice or path of disciplined growth.
- Related terms: practice, aspiration, surrender
- Use in guidance: Useful for framing gradual inner work.
- Cautions: Keep advice practical and non-coercive.

## Aspiration

- Traditions: Integral Yoga, devotional and yogic usage
- Short definition: Sincere upward call toward truth, light, or the Divine.
- Related terms: Agni, sincerity, prayer
- Use in guidance: Often a safe first movement for seekers.
- Cautions: Distinguish aspiration from restless ambition.

## Surrender

- Traditions: Bhakti, Integral Yoga, many devotional streams
- Short definition: Trustful self-offering to the Divine or truth.
- Related terms: bhakti, offering, humility
- Use in guidance: Helpful when control is exhausted or ego strain is high.
- Cautions: Never frame surrender as passivity before abuse or harm.

## Rejection

- Traditions: Integral Yoga
- Short definition: Conscious refusal of movements contrary to truth, peace, or aspiration.
- Related terms: aspiration, discernment, purification
- Use in guidance: Helps explain active participation in inner change.
- Cautions: Avoid shame-heavy or perfectionistic framing.

## Equality

- Traditions: Gita, Integral Yoga
- Short definition: Inner poise, evenness, or stable wideness amid dualities.
- Related terms: peace, witness, samata
- Use in guidance: Supports resilience and non-reactive action.
- Cautions: Do not confuse equality with numbness.

## Psychic Being

- Traditions: Integral Yoga
- Short definition: The evolving soul-center that turns the nature toward truth and the Divine.
- Related terms: soul, aspiration, sincerity
- Use in guidance: Useful when speaking of inner sincerity and soul-direction.
- Cautions: Explain clearly because the term is specialized.

## Jivatman

- Traditions: Vedantic and Integral Yoga usage
- Short definition: Individual Self or transcendent individual spirit.
- Related terms: Atman, psychic being
- Use in guidance: Helps distinguish soul-center from transcendent self-reference.
- Cautions: Clarify distinctions carefully.

## Supermind

- Traditions: Integral Yoga
- Short definition: Truth-consciousness beyond mind that unites knowledge and force.
- Related terms: overmind, transformation, divine gnosis
- Use in guidance: Relevant in advanced Integral Yoga philosophy.
- Cautions: Avoid casual or inflated use in ordinary guidance.

## Transformation

- Traditions: Siddha, Tantric, Integral Yoga, yogic streams
- Short definition: Deep change of consciousness and nature, not only belief or mood.
- Related terms: sadhana, Shakti, surrender
- Use in guidance: Helps shift focus from ideas to lived change.
- Cautions: Do not promise rapid attainment.

## Integral Yoga

- Traditions: Sri Aurobindo and The Mother
- Short definition: A path aiming at total transformation of life and consciousness.
- Related terms: aspiration, rejection, surrender, psychic being
- Use in guidance: Central for Aurobindonian materials and synthesis rules.
- Cautions: Do not blur it into generic self-development.
