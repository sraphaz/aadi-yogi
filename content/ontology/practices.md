# Practices

## Meditation

- Intent: cultivate stillness, awareness, and receptivity
- Use in guidance: general grounding and observation
- Cautions: tailor intensity to the seeker's condition

## Japa

- Intent: repetition of sacred sound or name
- Use in guidance: steadying the mind and heart
- Cautions: avoid prescribing specific mantras without context

## Mantra

- Intent: sacred sound used for concentration, invocation, or transformation
- Use in guidance: can support devotion, focus, and alignment
- Cautions: handle initiatory or restricted mantras carefully

## Aspiration

- Intent: sincere upward call toward truth or the Divine
- Use in guidance: safe starting movement in many cases
- Cautions: distinguish from restless ambition

## Surrender

- Intent: self-offering and trustful yielding to truth or the Divine
- Use in guidance: helpful when forceful control has become exhausting
- Cautions: never use to excuse passivity toward harm

## Self-Observation

- Intent: notice movements without immediate identification
- Use in guidance: useful for ego conflict and habit recognition
- Cautions: avoid hypervigilant self-surveillance

## Offering of Works

- Intent: consecrate action and outcomes beyond ego possession
- Use in guidance: central for work, duty, and action questions
- Cautions: do not erase practical responsibility

## Concentration

- Intent: gather scattered attention around a chosen center
- Use in guidance: supports practice stability and study
- Cautions: avoid strain and forced will

## Witness Consciousness

- Intent: establish inner distance from surface movements
- Use in guidance: helpful for confusion, reactivity, and discernment
- Cautions: do not turn witness into disengagement from life

## Devotion

- Intent: heart relation to the Divine
- Use in guidance: supports tenderness, trust, prayer, and surrender
- Cautions: distinguish devotion from emotional dependence

## Discernment

- Intent: separate truth from mixture, impulse, or illusion
- Use in guidance: essential across traditions
- Cautions: avoid coldness or superiority

## Silence

- Intent: quiet mind, speech, and reaction for deeper listening
- Use in guidance: helpful when answers should ripen slowly
- Cautions: not appropriate as avoidance in acute crisis
