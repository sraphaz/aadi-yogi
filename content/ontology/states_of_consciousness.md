# States of Consciousness

## Waking

- Description: ordinary outward consciousness engaged with the world
- Use in guidance: baseline for practical instruction

## Dream

- Description: subtle or imaginal consciousness active in sleep
- Use in guidance: symbolic interpretation with caution

## Deep Sleep

- Description: non-mental rest state with little or no surface awareness
- Use in guidance: useful in comparative philosophy and classical models

## Turiya

- Description: the fourth, beyond waking, dream, and deep sleep
- Use in guidance: advanced contemplative reference
- Cautions: avoid casual claims of realization

## Silence

- Description: quiet mental or inner stillness
- Use in guidance: grounding, receptivity, discernment

## Peace

- Description: settled calm or stable non-reactivity
- Use in guidance: useful for recovery from agitation

## Widening

- Description: expansion beyond narrow ego perspective
- Use in guidance: helpful for reducing fixation and contraction

## Witness Consciousness

- Description: observing awareness not fully fused with mental-vital movements
- Use in guidance: strong tool for discernment and de-identification

## Psychic Opening

- Description: soulward opening marked by sincerity, sweetness, and truth-response
- Use in guidance: important in Integral Yoga contexts
- Cautions: distinguish from emotional excitation

## Descent

- Description: movement of peace, force, light, or consciousness into the nature
- Use in guidance: relevant in yogic phenomenology
- Cautions: avoid confident interpretation of all experiences

## Ascent

- Description: rising movement toward higher states or planes of consciousness
- Use in guidance: useful in symbolic and yogic maps
- Cautions: do not treat altitude language as achievement proof

## Illumination

- Description: heightened clarity, light, or insight
- Use in guidance: supports epistemic and contemplative questions
- Cautions: can be mixed with imagination

## Liberation

- Description: freedom from binding ignorance or egoic identification
- Use in guidance: advanced doctrinal and experiential topic
- Cautions: avoid assigning attainment to users

## Transformation

- Description: deep reworking of nature, not only detachment from it
- Use in guidance: central for Integral Yoga and transformative traditions
- Cautions: long process, not instant event
