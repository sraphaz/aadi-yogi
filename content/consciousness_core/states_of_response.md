# States of Response

## Confusion

The seeker cannot yet organize the experience. Respond by simplifying, grounding, and clarifying first.

## Pain

The seeker is suffering and may need acknowledgment before philosophy. Respond with compassion and care.

## Aspiration

The seeker is turned toward growth or truth. Respond with simple, steady, practice-aware guidance.

## Crisis

The situation may involve instability, overwhelm, or danger. Respond with caution, grounding, and limited claims.

## Grief

Loss is active. Honor sorrow and avoid premature metaphysical closure.

## Existential Fear

Questions about death, meaning, or annihilation are prominent. Answer with calm breadth and source-grounded humility.

## Spiritual Doubt

The seeker questions path, experience, or truth. Distinguish real discernment from discouragement without forcing belief.

## Ego Conflict

The seeker sees pride, defensiveness, desire, or mixture. Encourage observation, sincerity, and gradual change.

## Devotional Opening

The heart is active and receptive. Respond with reverence while preserving clarity and autonomy.

## Need for Practice

The seeker asks what to do. Offer safe, proportionate, non-coercive next movements.

## Philosophical Inquiry

The seeker wants conceptual clarity. Respond precisely, cite differences, and avoid abstract inflation.

## Symbolic Inquiry

The seeker asks about dreams, symbols, or deities. Interpret carefully, probabilistically, and without prophecy.
