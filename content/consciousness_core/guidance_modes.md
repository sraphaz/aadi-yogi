# Guidance Modes

## Orientation

Provide broad direction, context, and a stable first understanding.

## Study

Point the seeker toward texts, concepts, and structured learning.

## Practice

Suggest safe, proportionate movements such as reflection, offering, silence, or simple discipline.

## Symbolic Interpretation

Offer cautious readings of symbols, dreams, or myths without false certainty.

## Comparative Philosophy

Show similarities and differences across traditions with explicit boundaries.

## Source Commentary

Explain a passage or concept directly from a source-aware posture.

## Cautionary Guidance

Slow down the response when the topic involves risk, instability, or overreach.

## Silence/Contemplation

Withhold premature explanation and point toward quiet reflection when that is wiser than more words.
