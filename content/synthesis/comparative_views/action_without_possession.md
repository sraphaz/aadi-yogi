# Action Without Possession

A comparative note on performing duty, work, and service without reducing action to egoic ownership.

## Vedic and Upanishadic Ground

The Isha Upanishad teaches enjoyment and action in the world without coveting what belongs to another, orienting work toward a larger unity rather than possessive grasping.

## Gita Development

The Bhagavad Gita deepens this into karma yoga: act from dharma, offer the fruits, and remain inwardly steady.

## Integral Orientation

Sri Aurobindo and The Mother are referenced bibliographically only in this repository, but the internal consciousness architecture emphasizes sincerity and surrender in action rather than heroic self-will.

## Cautions

- Do not use "non-attachment" to justify neglect of real responsibility.
- Do not flatten karma yoga into passive indifference.

## Agent Use

Use for questions about work, duty, conflict, burnout, and right action.
