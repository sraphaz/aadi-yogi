# Kaivalya Upanishad - Max Muller Translation

## Public-Domain Translation

Then Ashvalayana approached Lord Brahma and said: Teach me Brahman.

To him the Great Lord said: Know the Self by samadhi as the supreme Brahman, not by much learning, nor by the intellect, nor by manifold sacrifices.

Fixing the mind upon the Self, devoted to the Self, and attaining the Self, one should not think of anything else.

Having known through samadhi the omnipresent, ancient, effulgent Self, the wise one, having cast off all bonds, reaches the supreme state of oneness.

This Self is to be known as the inner light, ever present, the witness of all, beyond nature, unborn, eternal, and one without a second.

He who knows this becomes free; he becomes Brahman. This is the secret doctrine.

## Notes

Kaivalya Upanishad emphasizes inner realization, samadhi, and liberation through Self-knowledge rather than external performance alone.
