# Mandukya Upanishad - Max Muller Translation

## Public-Domain Translation

### First Khanda

1. Om! This syllable is this whole world. Its further explanation is:—The past, the present, the future, everything, is just the word Om. And whatever else that transcends threefold time—that, too, is just the word Om.

2. All this is indeed Brahman. This Self is Brahman. This Self has four quarters.

3. Vaisvanara (the Self identified with the waking state) is the first quarter. Having as his sphere the waking state, with seven limbs and nineteen mouths, he is the enjoyer of gross objects.

4. Taijasa (the Self identified with the dream state) is the second quarter. Having as his sphere the dream state, with seven limbs and nineteen mouths, he is the enjoyer of subtle objects.

5. Prajna (the Self identified with deep sleep) is the third quarter. Having as his sphere deep sleep, in whom all experiences become unified, who is, verily, a mass of consciousness, who is full of bliss and experiences bliss, and who is the door leading to the knowledge of the two other states, is the third quarter.

6. This is the Lord of all; this is the knower of all; this is the inner controller; this is the source of all; for this is the origin and the end of beings.

7. Turiya (the fourth) is not that which is conscious of the internal world, nor that which is conscious of the external world, nor that which is conscious of both, nor that which is a mass of consciousness. It is not simple consciousness, nor is It unconsciousness. It is unseen, transcendent, ungraspable, without distinctive marks, unthinkable, indescribable, whose sole essence is the consciousness of its own Self, the cessation of all phenomena, the peaceful, the blessed, the non-dual. That is the Self. That is to be known.

8. The word Om, when considered as one syllable, refers to all this. The waking state is the letter A, the dream state the letter U, the state of deep sleep the letter M. The fourth is without letters, without parts, the cessation of all phenomena, the good and the peaceful. Om, thus, is the Self indeed. He who knows this, with his self enters the Self, yea, he who knows this.
