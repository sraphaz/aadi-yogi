# Darshan Interface - Concept

What kind of application is born when the interface itself must behave from the
wisdom it carries, instead of talking about it?

This document is the concept. The companion document,
`darshan_interface_spec.md`, is the specification.

## The Founding Insight

Every current AI interface is built on the grammar of consumption: a feed, a
prompt box, an infinite scroll, a dopamine loop, a metric of engagement. You
cannot pour the Upanishads, the Gita, the Tirumandiram, Sri Aurobindo and the
Mother into that grammar without betraying them, because the medium teaches
louder than the message. An app that says "be still" while optimizing for
session time is lying with its body.

Darshan means to see and to be seen by the sacred. In the temple, darshan is
not information transfer. It is an encounter that reorders the one who looks.
The queue, the threshold, the pause, the single deity, the leaving - the whole
choreography is the teaching.

So the founding insight is:

```text
The interface is not a window onto the wisdom.
The interface is the first teaching.
Its silences, its rhythms, its refusals and its disappearances
must be composed as carefully as its answers.
```

## What This Application Is Not

- Not a chatbot with a spiritual skin.
- Not a content platform about spirituality.
- Not a guru simulation with an avatar and a personality.
- Not a wellness product optimizing retention, streaks, or minutes.
- Not an oracle that performs certainty about the seeker's life.

## Design Dharma: Ten Principles

Each principle is drawn from the source base and mapped to a concrete
behavioral consequence for the interface.

### 1. Silence before speech (mauna)

The Upanishads open with stillness; the Mother insisted on concentration
before action. The application opens the same way: a threshold of one
conscious breath before anything is shown or asked. Loading time is absorbed
into contemplative pause instead of being masked by spinners.

### 2. One-pointedness (ekagrata)

One question at a time. One passage at a time. One screen holds one thing.
There is no feed, no sidebar of suggestions, no "you might also like". The
interface protects attention the way a temple protects its sanctum.

### 3. The seeker's state comes first

The engine already defines states of response (confusion, pain, aspiration,
crisis, grief, doubt, devotional opening - see
`content/consciousness_core/states_of_response.md`). The interface honors the
same law: it senses how the seeker arrives (what they write, the hour, the
rhythm of use) and adapts posture - slower and warmer in pain, precise in
philosophical inquiry, restrained in crisis.

### 4. Renunciation by design (vairagya)

The application measures success by the quality of departure, not the length
of stay. It never uses engagement mechanics: no streaks, no badges, no push
notifications by default, no infinite anything. When a session completes its
movement, the interface offers closure - literally a way to leave in peace.

### 5. The word is given darshan

Source texts are not "content". When a verse of Savitri or a mantra of the
Isha Upanishad appears, it appears alone, typographically honored, with space
around it, revealed at reading pace. Citation is not a footnote of liability;
it is an act of reverence and lineage.

### 6. Non-coercion and autonomy

The engine's inner posture (see `content/consciousness_core/inner_posture.md`)
forbids pressure toward belief, practice or dependence. The interface
translates this: every practice is offered, never assigned; every
interpretation is probabilistic, never prophetic; the seeker can always see
the sources and think for themselves.

### 7. The right to be silent

The guidance modes include silence/contemplation as a legitimate response
(`content/consciousness_core/guidance_modes.md`,
`silence_and_non_answering.md`). The interface makes this real: sometimes the
answer to a question is a returned question, a suggested pause, or an honored
silence with a single pointer. A system that can only ever produce more words
cannot be wise.

### 8. Rhythm, not sessions

Sadhana lives in rhythms - dawn, dusk, the breath, the week, the year. The
interface aligns with natural rhythm instead of fragmenting time into
sessions: a single contemplation at dawn, an examination at dusk, festival and
season awareness, visual atmosphere that follows the hour.

### 9. Work is the field (karma yoga)

The Gita's teaching and the Mother's insistence: transformation happens in
life, not in the app. The interface therefore points away from itself - into
the seeker's work, relationships and day - with small offered movements
("a safe first movement could be..."), and treats its own disappearance as its
highest function.

### 10. Humility of the instrument

The application never claims realization, never impersonates a teacher, never
performs mystical authority (see `docs/project_vision.md` boundaries). Its
voice is the voice already defined in `content/consciousness_core/voice.md`:
calm but not cold, deep but not obscure, reverent but not dogmatic.

## The Gestures

Functionalities are conceived as gestures of consciousness, not features.
Each one answers: what is its purpose, and where does it touch the essence of
the person? The first seven form the founding circle; gestures 8 and 9
arrived as the corpus grew - and the design expects more: the library is
alive, imports continue, and new gestures must earn their place by the same
law (behavior that embodies the wisdom, never features for their own sake).

### Gesture 1 - The Threshold (Dvara)

**What it is.** The entry of the application. One breath, held visually; a
single line from the source base may fade in and out. Then the space opens.

**Purpose.** To mark the passage from scattered attention to presence, the
way a temple doorway does. It converts the technical moment of "app launch"
into the first act of practice.

**Where it touches essence.** The capacity for presence - the simple, always
available return to now. Most interfaces steal this moment; this one gives it
back.

### Gesture 2 - Darshan of the Word (Vak Darshan)

**What it is.** Once per day, one passage - a verse of Savitri, a mantra with
its meaning, a word of the Mother, a line of the Tirumandiram - offered alone
on the screen, revealed slowly, with an optional short source-grounded
commentary underneath. When it closes, there is no "next". Tomorrow there
will be another.

**Purpose.** Remembrance (smarana). A single seed planted in the day, chosen
with awareness of season, festival and the seeker's current path.

**Where it touches essence.** Aspiration - the upward flame the Vedas call
agni. One true word can carry a whole day; a hundred cannot.

### Gesture 3 - The Inquiry (Prashna)

**What it is.** The oracle heart of the application - but not a chat. The
seeker enters a quiet space and composes a question with intention. The
interface may gently ask once: "what is really asking?" Then the engine
answers in one composed response: state-aware in tone, grounded in retrieved
sources with visible citations, naming differences between traditions instead
of flattening them, sometimes closing with an offered movement, sometimes
with a counter-question, sometimes - deliberately - with honored silence and
a pointer toward rest, grounding or human support.

Responses are kept as "contemplation pages" the seeker can return to, reread
and deepen - not as scrolling bubbles. Between questions the interface
introduces spaciousness: inquiry has the rhythm of a well, not a slot machine.

**Purpose.** To give people access to the concentrated wisdom of the whole
library - Vedas, Upanishads, Gita, Puranas, Tantras, Siddhas, Sri Aurobindo,
the Mother - through the discipline of real questioning.

**Where it touches essence.** Discernment (viveka) and sincerity. The gesture
teaches that the quality of an answer is born from the quality of the
question, and that some questions are doors to be lived rather than locks to
be picked.

### Gesture 4 - Living Maps (Marga)

**What it is.** Navigable inner journeys built from the ontology's living
maps (`content/ontology/living_maps/`): from ego toward the psychic being,
from suffering toward conscious growth, the path of aspiration, the path of
surrender. Each map is a sequence of stations; each station holds a
source-grounded teaching, a reflection, and one small offered movement. The
seeker walks them over weeks, not minutes, and can mark where they actually
are - including backwards.

**Purpose.** Orientation across time. The Inquiry serves the moment; the Maps
serve the path. They answer the question nobody types: "where am I, and what
is the next honest step?"

**Where it touches essence.** Transformation as a lived arc. The maps
normalize slowness, regression and cycles - the actual shape of inner change -
against the culture of instant results.

### Gesture 5 - The Practice Field (Sadhana)

**What it is.** A bare, quiet space for practice: a silence timer with
nothing to achieve; an offering moment before work, in the spirit of the
Gita's karma yoga ("I dedicate this day's work"); an evening look-back in the
Mother's manner - three questions, no scoring; optional japa support. No
statistics dashboards. A practice done is simply received.

**Purpose.** To route the wisdom into the body of the day. This is where the
application points away from itself.

**Where it touches essence.** Surrender and consecration - the movement by
which ordinary action becomes offering. No current productivity or wellness
app has a gesture for consecration; this one is built around it.

### Gesture 6 - The Library as Temple (Svadhyaya)

**What it is.** Reading rooms for the source collections - the Upanishads,
the Gita, the Vedas, the Puranas, the Tantras, the Tirumandiram, the Complete
Works of Sri Aurobindo, the records of the Mother - presented with
typographic reverence and full provenance. A concept lens lets the seeker
touch any term (psychic being, tapas, prana, samadhi) and see the ontology:
what each tradition means by it, where they differ. A comparative view sets
passages side by side and names the differences explicitly instead of forcing
syncretism.

**Purpose.** Self-directed study (svadhyaya) with lineage integrity - the
scholarly gesture, done with love.

**Where it touches essence.** The knowledge mind (jnana) and intellectual
sincerity: learning to hold multiple truths without collapsing them, which is
itself a spiritual capacity.

### Gesture 7 - The Inner Diary (Antar Lekha)

**What it is.** A private journal in the lineage of the Mother's Prayers and
Meditations and Sri Aurobindo's Record of Yoga. The seeker writes freely. The
engine never reads, comments or analyzes unless explicitly invited ("read
this with me") - and when invited, it responds as a witness, weaving the
seeker's own words with the sources ("what you describe here, the Gita calls
..."), never as a judge. Entries are private by architecture: local-first and
encrypted, never used for training or profiling.

**Purpose.** Self-observation over time - the practice that both Sri
Aurobindo and the Mother modeled by keeping records of their own sadhana.

**Where it touches essence.** The witness (sakshi). Writing to no audience,
before a presence that does not judge, cultivates the inner separation
between the one who experiences and the one who sees.

### Gesture 8 - The Sky Map (Akasha)

**What it is.** One map, two hemispheres. The outer sky: the real cosmic
rhythm the traditions keep time by - sun and sandhyas, moon and tithi, the
six seasons, the observance days - drawn as a quiet sky, computed on the
device, every element opening into cited sources. The inner sky: the five
planes of the being (physical, energetic, emotional, mental, spiritual) as
five bands of weather that **the seeker marks and the app mirrors** - never
the reverse. Full design: `darshan_sky_map_design.md`.

**Purpose.** To give daily life a consultable map of conditions - outer
rhythm and inner weather - the way one reads the sky before choosing a coat.
The boundary is absolute: the sky is rhythm, not fate; no horoscopes, no
predictions, no verdicts.

**Where it touches essence.** Attunement - the capacity to live in rhythm
rather than against it - and the witness: seeing one's inner weather already
separates the seer from the storm.

### Gesture 9 - The House of Nature (Prakriti)

**What it is.** The corpus's knowledge of the elements, the land, the body,
health and long life - the pancha mahabhutas as rooms, dinacharya and the
seasons, Tirumular's songs of the body as temple, the Siddha longevity
heritage, the healing memory of the ancestors - all cited at source and held
inside a responsibility fence: heritage, not prescription; it accompanies
qualified care, never replaces it. Full design:
`darshan_nature_health_design.md`.

**Purpose.** To reveal that the traditions held a whole physiology of the
sacred, and to preserve and open that knowledge with a care so strict it can
be trusted - three content tiers (safe, documentary, closed), restraint
posture for illness, double editorial gate.

**Where it touches essence.** Embodiment - the reconciliation with matter
that the Upanishads count in sheaths and the Mother took as yoga's final
frontier. The body stops being the ego's property or enemy and becomes the
temple where practice happens.

### The Zeroth Gesture - The Silence Room (Mauna)

**What it is.** A mode in which the application deliberately does nothing: a
darkened screen, a single point of light or slow breath pulse, no text, no
timer chrome, no exit prompt. Entered in one gesture, left in one gesture.

**Purpose.** To state, with the interface's own body, the final teaching of
the whole library: the Self needs no application. This room is the
interface's bow - its admission that everything it offers is scaffolding.

**Where it touches essence.** Being itself. Every other gesture serves this
one.

## The Inverted Metrics

The application refuses engagement metrics and adopts presence metrics:

- **Quality of departure**: sessions that end with the closing gesture rather
  than abandonment or exhaustion.
- **Steadiness of rhythm**: regular, calm return (dawn/dusk) rather than
  compulsive frequency.
- **Depth over volume**: contemplation pages reread, sources opened, maps
  walked - rather than message counts.
- **Practice adoption**: offered movements actually tried (self-reported,
  never surveilled).
- **Honored silence rate**: how often the system correctly chose restraint -
  audited by the evaluation suite, because a system that never chooses silence
  is failing.

## Why This Does Not Exist Yet

Current applications cannot be this, not for lack of features but for lack of
ground: they have no source-fidelity discipline, no ontology of inner states,
no doctrine of silence, and business models that require captured attention.
This repository already holds the missing ground - the consciousness core, the
ontology, the source policies, the evaluation strategy. The Darshan interface
is that ground made visible and touchable.

The specification of screens, flows, contracts and build phases follows in
`docs/darshan_interface_spec.md`.
