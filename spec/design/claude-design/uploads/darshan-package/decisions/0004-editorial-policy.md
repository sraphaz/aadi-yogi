# ADR 0004 - Editorial Policy: Daily Word Curation and D1 Review

Status: accepted (decided from the consciousness core; creators calibrate)

## Context

Two editorial mechanisms need policy before implementation: the daily
Darshan of the Word curation (RF-003) and the D1 plain-meaning layer
(RF-018). The journey flagged both (`editorial_policy`).

## What the Consciousness Says

- **Source fidelity** (`project_vision.md`): no invented citations; the
  most-read layer must be the most-reviewed layer.
- **Integral breadth** (`essence.md`): the corpus honors Vedic fire,
  Upanishadic Self, the Gita's action, Puranic imagination, Siddha and
  Tantric transformation, Sri Aurobindo and the Mother - curation must
  breathe with all of them, not settle into a favorite.
- **Restraint**: intense texts (occult techniques, extreme tapas, death
  meditations) are not dawn material for an unknown reader.

## Decision

### Daily Word curation

1. Selection priority: (1) observance/festival of the day, (2) season, (3)
   the seeker's active living map or posture, (4) a slow rotation that
   crosses all traditions in the corpus within each lunar month.
2. The candidate pool is a curated set marked `daily_word_eligible` in
   passage metadata - eligibility excludes restraint-flagged and
   documentary-tier passages by construction.
3. Words are drafted in monthly batches (a lunar month ahead), reviewed
   before publication; the pipeline is data, so the app never generates a
   daily word live.
4. Interim ownership: the creators approve each monthly batch until a
   dedicated editor exists; the batch file records reviewer and date.

### D1 plain meanings

1. Drafted in batch by the engine under the voice rules, one per passage,
   cited; stored as corpus content (never live generation).
2. Published only after human review; unreviewed drafts render with the
   "machine-drafted, pending review" mark and never enter the daily word
   pool or Inquiry quotations.
3. Review order follows reading traffic: most-encountered passages first
   (daily word pool, situation door passages, map stations).

## Consequences

- The daily word and D1 layers become reviewable, versionable corpus
  artifacts with a visible provenance trail - the same discipline as the
  texts themselves.
